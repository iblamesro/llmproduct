# GDPR Article 35 - Data Protection Impact Assessment

## When is a DPIA Required?

A Data Protection Impact Assessment (DPIA) is mandatory when processing is likely to result in high risk to the rights and freedoms of natural persons, particularly when using new technologies.

## Situations Requiring DPIA

1. **Systematic and extensive evaluation** of personal aspects based on automated processing
2. **Large-scale processing** of special categories of data or personal data relating to criminal convictions
3. **Systematic monitoring** of a publicly accessible area on a large scale

## Content of DPIA

The assessment must contain at least:

### 1. Description of Processing Operations
- Nature, scope, context, and purposes of processing
- Categories of personal data
- Recipients of data
- Storage periods

### 2. Necessity and Proportionality Assessment
- Purposes of processing and means
- Assessment of necessity
- Assessment of proportionality

### 3. Risk Assessment
Evaluation of risks to rights and freedoms of data subjects:
- Source of risk
- Nature of risk
- Severity of risk
- Likelihood of risk

### 4. Mitigation Measures
- Security measures
- Safeguards
- Security mechanisms
- Procedures to demonstrate compliance

## DPIA Process

1. **Screening**: Determine if DPIA is necessary
2. **Describe**: Document the processing operation
3. **Consult**: Seek views of data subjects where appropriate
4. **Assess**: Identify and assess risks
5. **Identify**: Determine measures to mitigate risks
6. **Sign off**: Approve and record the DPIA
7. **Monitor**: Keep DPIA under review

## Prior Consultation with Supervisory Authority

Required when:
- DPIA indicates high risk
- Controller cannot identify sufficient measures to mitigate risk
- Residual risk remains high despite mitigation efforts

## Supervisory Authority Response

Must provide written advice within 8 weeks (extendable to 14 weeks):
- Whether processing complies with GDPR
- Remediation measures
- Use of standard contractual clauses
- Authorization for binding corporate rules

## Banking Sector Specific Considerations

### AI and Automated Decision Making
Banks using AI for:
- Credit scoring
- Fraud detection
- Customer profiling
- Risk assessment

Must conduct DPIA addressing:
- Algorithm transparency
- Explanation rights (Article 22)
- Human oversight mechanisms
- Bias detection and mitigation

### Large-Scale Processing
Banks typically process data on large scale:
- Customer databases
- Transaction histories
- Cross-border transfers
- Third-party data sharing

### Special Categories of Data
Financial data may reveal:
- Health information (insurance, medical loans)
- Trade union membership (payroll deductions)
- Political opinions (donations)
- Religious beliefs (charity contributions)

## Documentation Requirements

DPIA must be:
- **Written**: Formal documented assessment
- **Comprehensive**: Cover all aspects of processing
- **Updated**: Review when circumstances change
- **Accessible**: Available for supervisory authority inspection

## Review and Update Triggers

DPIA must be reviewed when:
- Processing operation changes significantly
- New risks emerge
- Technology evolves
- Legal requirements change
- Every 2-3 years as best practice

## Accountability Measures

Controllers must:
- Maintain records of all DPIAs
- Document decision-making processes
- Keep evidence of supervisory authority consultations
- Demonstrate compliance with Article 35

## Integration with Risk Management

DPIA should align with:
- Enterprise Risk Management (ERM)
- Operational Risk Framework
- Compliance Management System
- Internal Audit Programs
